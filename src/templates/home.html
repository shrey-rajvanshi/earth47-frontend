How it works
1
Solar panels
Solar panels are the most important component of your rooftop system. They convert the sunlight falling on your roof to DC electricity, which is then fed to the inverter
2
Inverter
Inverters are the heart of a solar system. The DC electricity produced by solar panels can't directly be used in our homes. The inverter converts it into usable AC electricity to power our appliances
3
Distribution box
This box is the junction where all the load from the house and wiring from the inverter are connected. It has a bunch of safety features like fuses to cut supply if the current exceeds a safe amount
4
Load
This refers to all the electrical appliances in your house like fans, bulbs, TVs, ACs, Fridge, Washing machine etc which consume electricity
5
Net meter
This special meter is capable of running in reverse. It keeps track of difference of electricity generated by your solar system and electricity you use from the grid (like at night). So you only pay if you use more than you produce!
6
Grid
The electrical grid which currently powers your home- with all its wires, poles, transformers and power stations
Earth 47 always designs systems to ensure that your net consumption from the grid is ZERO. The energy you use from the grid at night is cancelled out by the excess energy you generate during the day.
